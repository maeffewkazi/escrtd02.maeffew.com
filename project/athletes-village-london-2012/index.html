01 An exciting start

Being involved in the redevelopment project for the London 2012 Olympics was both exciting and challenging.

We were the last architects brought onto the project and faced a combination of tight scheduling, significant building constraints and complex stakeholder approval.

Our contribution combined a ‘can-do’ pragmatism with a principled focus on the end users’ needs. This not only overcame the very challenging building constraints but also resulted in something unique and long-lasting: a new typology of family social housing that prioritises external space, daylight and layers of privacy.

02 A mix of scales

Vitally, we insisted on a mix of scale otherwise absent from the rest of the Olympic Village.

This provided 242 homes that combined vertical three-story houses and apartments going up to ten floors, with apartments ranging from one-bedroom to four- bedroom units.

We consciously made bigger balconies and bigger windows and configured interiors without long corridors, which waste valuable space. We used dual-aspect apartments where possible and opted for an external cladding system in terracotta that provided colour and sustainability, rejecting precast concrete in the process. Finally, we created commercial space at the base of the building, offering mixed use, which we feel is vital to any new urban setting.

03 Putting residents first

At every stage we prioritised the needs of the eventual residents – not simply the athletes using the buildings for the summer of 2012 - but individuals and families who would be setting up their homes as part of the Olympics legacy.

The result is a project with its own identity and a series of buildings that we hope can be enjoyed for years to come. Testament to that has been the success of the East Village, a new community that has developed from the former athletes’ village. The neighbourhood now has a school, health centre, shops and gyms. Transport links are excellent and thousands of tenants enjoy the proximity to the Olympic Park in what is a thriving, active community.
